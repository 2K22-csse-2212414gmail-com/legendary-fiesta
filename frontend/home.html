LUNAR CRATER & BOULDER DETECTION

Automated Detection of Lunar Surface Features using YOLOv8, YOLO-NAS & High-Resolution OHRC Images from ISRO’s Chandrayaan-2

🚀 YOLOv8 Object Detection | Deep Learning | Space Vision | ISRO Dataset 🚀

ABSTRACT

This work investigates the automated detection of lunar craters and boulders using advanced Computer Vision (CV) techniques, specifically leveraging the YOLOv11 and YOLO-NAS object detection models on high-resolution images from ISRO’s Chandrayaan-2 Orbiter High-Resolution Camera (OHRC).

The study focuses on training deep learning models, evaluating mean Average Precision (mAP), and optimizing detection accuracy for planetary exploration and mission planning.

INTRODUCTION

With the development of science and technology, space probes capture massive planetary images that provide valuable geological insights. Detecting craters and boulders automatically is essential for understanding impact history, landing site selection, and surface evolution.

Single-stage object detection models like YOLO offer high-speed detection with strong accuracy, making them ideal for large-scale planetary image analysis.

LUNAR SURFACE SAMPLE

The most fundamental elements of space surfaces are craters and boulders. Craters result from meteor impacts, while boulders originate from erosion, landslides, and surface fragmentation processes.
